Jin Wu（吴荩/吳藎）


Jin Wu, Tenured Full Professor,
PhD in Electronic and Computer Engineering (HKUST),
Bachelor in Applied Science (UESTC)

I have been with University of Science and Technology Beijing (USTB, 北京科技大學, 211工程) since 2025, as a Full Professor (全職正教授), in cooperation with Prof. Wei He (賀威 教授, IEEE Fellow, Associate Editor of IEEE Transactions on Robotics, IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering, IEEE Transactions on Control Systems Technology, IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems).

Currently, I serve for academic journals, such as Technical Editor of IEEE/ASME Transactions on Mechatronics, Editor of Scientific Reports (Nature Publishing), Associate Editor of AppliedMath.

My research is Pose/Attitude Estimation in Aerospace Engineering and Computational Robotics. Specifically the topic falls within the algorithmic foundations and related mechatronic/circuit/embedded/FPGA/ASIC systems design/control using numerical optimization, deep learning, and high-performance computing, with applications in aerospace engineering, space sciences, industrial/mobile robotics, computer vision, structural chemistry, computer animation, and consumer electronics. I started C++ programming since 2004, the MFC programming in 2006, and the Qt programming in 2009. I am the inventor of the world's first ultra low-cost/low-power circuit for 3-D registration (FA3R, Independent Author, IEEE TIM, 2019). I am also the author of several popular AHRS/pose estimation algorithms: FCF (First Author, IEEE Sensors J., 2016), FLAE (First Author, IEEE TASE, 2017), QPEP (First Author, IEEE TRO, 2021). My research works are found in PX4 Autopilot, Python AHRS SDK, libigl (Computer Animation), OpenCV, etc. In collaboration with my comarade Xiangcheng Hu (HKUST), we have made considerable achievements in academia and engineering, on LiDAR, IMU, and visual SLAM. Related repos are available: LIO_SAM_6AXIS (GNSS-Aided LiDAR SLAM, 600+ Stars), PALoc (IEEE TMECH, Groundtruth Generation for LiDAR SLAM, 200+ Stars), AM-Align (Accelerometer-Magnetometer Calibration)

I obtained my Bachelor's degree from University of Electronic Science and Technolology of China (UESTC, 電子科技大學, founded in 1956, QS Ranking 456, 2024), supervised by Prof. Zebo Zhou and Prof. Rui Li, co-supervised by Prof. Bin Gao and Prof. Yuhua Cheng. In 2013, I was a visiting student with Groep T, Katholieke Universiteit Leuven (KU Leuven, 比利時天主教魯汶大學, a 575 year-old university founded by Martin V in 1425, QS Ranking 76, 2024). I received my PhD degree in Department of Electronic and Computer Engineering, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST, 香港科技大學, a young University founded in 1991, QS Ranking 60, 2024), Hong Kong SAR, China, in December 2024, under the supervision of Prof. Wei Zhang（張薇 教授）. Since 2018, I have been closely working with Prof. Chengxi Zhang (張承璽 教授, Jiangnan University), Prof. Chong Li (李崇 教授, Ocean University of China), and Prof. Yi Jiang (姜藝 教授, CityU of Hong Kong).

I worked as a shareholder of several companies of unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) services since 2012. Previously, I worked as an engineer in Tencent Robotics X (騰訊機器人), under the administration of Dr. Zhengyou Zhang. I worked as an active educator of open-source UAV systems like PX4 and ArduPilot on embedded programming and optimization in China from 2015 to 2018.

Till now, my related research works have been reported in several journal and conference papers, which include top-tier publications, such as 《IEEE Transactions on Robotics (T-RO)》, 《IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems (T-CAS)》, 《International Journal of Robotics Research (IJRR)》, 《IEEE/ASME Transactions on Mechatronics (T-MECH》, 《IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering (T-ASE》, 《IEEE Transactions on Cybernetics (T-CYB)》, 《IEEE Transactions on Aerospace and Electronic Systems (T-AES)》, 《AIAA Journal of Guidance Control and Dynamics (JGCD)》, and 《IFAC Automatica》. I have been selected as the Stanford Top 2% Scientist (斯坦福世界前2%科學家) by Stanford University and published by Elsevier Inc., in 2019, 2020, 2021, 2022, 2023 and 2024, according to my contributions in Aerospace Engineering and Robotics. As of 2025, my research work has received over 3400 citations, summarized by Google Scholar. Among worldwide Google Scholar subject rankings (Jun 2025), I ranked in 36th in "3D Reconstruction", 79th in "SLAM", 17th in "Symbolic Computation", and 64th in "Aerospace Engineering".
